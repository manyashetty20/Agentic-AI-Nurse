Symptom: Non-Traumatic Chest Pain

Chest pain is a high-risk chief complaint.

1. Myocardial Infarction (Heart Attack) - HIGH RISK:
   - Definition: Blockage of blood flow to the heart.
   - Symptoms: Crushing pressure, pain in the center or left of the chest, pain radiating to the jaw, left arm, or back.
   - Associated Symptoms: Shortness of breath, nausea, sweating, lightheadedness.

2. Aortic Dissection - HIGH RISK:
   - Definition: A tear in the inner layer of the aorta.
   - Symptoms: Sudden, severe, "tearing" or "ripping" chest pain that may radiate to the back.

3. Pulmonary Embolism (PE) - HIGH RISK:
   - Definition: A blood clot in the lungs.
   - Symptoms: Sudden sharp chest pain, shortness of breath, cough.
   - Risk Factors: Recent surgery, long travel, history of clots.
