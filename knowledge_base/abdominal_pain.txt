Symptom: Abdominal Pain

Abdominal pain requires careful location and character assessment.

Appendicitis (HIGH RISK):

Location: Starts generalized, migrates to lower right quadrant (LRQ).

Character: Constant, worsening pain.

Associated Symptoms: Nausea, vomiting, low-grade fever.

Red Flag: Pain that is severe and localizing.

Gastritis / Ulcer:

Location: Upper center (epigastric).

Character: Burning, gnawing, often related to food intake (worse or better after eating).

Associated Symptoms: Indigestion, heartburn.

Gastroenteritis (Stomach Flu):

Location: Diffuse, generalized cramping.

Character: Cramping, colicky pain.

Associated Symptoms: Diarrhea, vomiting, often recent exposure to sick individuals.

Kidney Stones:

Location: Flank (side) radiating downwards to the groin.

Character: Excruciating, intermittent waves of pain (colicky).

Associated Symptoms: Blood in urine, nausea, inability to find a comfortable position.

Red Flag: Severe pain with inability to pass urine.
