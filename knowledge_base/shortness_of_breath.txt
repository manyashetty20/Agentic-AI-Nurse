Symptom: Shortness of Breath (Dyspnea)

Dyspnea is a high-priority symptom that requires assessment of onset and associated symptoms.

Asthma / COPD Exacerbation:

Onset: Often chronic, but acute episodes triggered by irritants or infection.

Associated Symptoms: Wheezing, cough, chest tightness.

Congestive Heart Failure (CHF):

Onset: Often gradual, worsening over time.

Associated Symptoms: Swelling in the feet/ankles (edema), cough, fatigue, waking up short of breath (PND).

Red Flag: Rapidly worsening edema or shortness of breath at rest.

Pulmonary Embolism (PE) - HIGH RISK:

Onset: Sudden and severe.

Associated Symptoms: Sharp chest pain (pleuritic), cough, low oxygen saturation.

Red Flag: Acute onset of SOB with sharp chest pain.

Pneumonia:

Onset: Gradual, worsening cough over days.

Associated Symptoms: Fever, productive cough (phlegm), chest pain when taking a deep breath.
