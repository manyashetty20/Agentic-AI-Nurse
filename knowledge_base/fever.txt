Symptom: Fever

Fever is a non-specific symptom, but its associated symptoms help localize the infection.

Viral Infection (e.g., Common Cold/Flu):

Fever Pattern: Low-grade to moderate (99°F - 102°F).

Associated Symptoms: Congestion, sore throat, generalized body aches, headache, fatigue.

Bacterial Infection (e.g., Sinusitis, Strep Throat):

Fever Pattern: Often higher (102°F+), or persistent.

Associated Symptoms: Localized pain (throat, ear, sinus pressure), colored mucus.

Sepsis (HIGH RISK):

Fever Pattern: Can be high or low (hypothermia).

Associated Symptoms: Confusion, rapid heart rate, low blood pressure, pale/clammy skin, shivering.

Red Flag: Shaking chills, confusion, or inability to stay awake.
