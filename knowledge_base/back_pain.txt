Symptom: Back Pain

Back pain is assessed based on location, radiation, and neurological signs.

Musculoskeletal Strain (Most Common):

Location: Localized to the muscles (e.g., lower back/lumbar region).

Character: Dull ache, stiffness, worse with movement.

Mechanism: Often lifting or poor posture.

Sciatica / Herniated Disc:

Location: Lower back, radiating down one leg below the knee.

Character: Sharp, shooting, electric pain.

Associated Symptoms: Numbness, tingling, weakness in the leg or foot.

Red Flag: Bowel or bladder incontinence (requires immediate attention).

Vertebral Compression Fracture:

Location: Mid-back or lower back.

Character: Sudden, severe, sharp pain, worse with standing/walking.

Risk Factors: Osteoporosis, trauma, older age.

Kidney Infection (Pyelonephritis):

Location: Unilateral (one-sided) flank pain.

Associated Symptoms: High fever, chills, nausea, painful or frequent urination.

Red Flag: High fever accompanying flank pain.
